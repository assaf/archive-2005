What are Textags?

Textags are simple textual markups that use the first word in a line to indicate the meaning of what follows. They make it incredibly easy to create posts with rich data: events, people, tags.

Textags are plain text, they work in your browser, your e-mail, you can SMS them or write them on a piece of paper. But on the Web they acquire special formatting that software (search engines, browsers) can understand.

The rules are very simple. The Textag comes first on the line, followed by a colon and all the relevant information. Textags can be lower or upper case.

The tags: Textag

Use the tags: Textag to tag a post with one or more tags. Multiple tags can be separated with spaces or commas. If you want the tag to include spaces, enclose it in quotes. For example:

tags: textags microformat wp-plugins

The links: Textag

For quick links use the links: Textag. It takes a URL, which you can follow with a description. For example:

link: http://www.labnotes.org/textags Check out Textags

Event Textags

An event is defined by the Textag when:, which tells you when the event will happen. For example:

when: September 5, 2005
when: 9/5/2005 3pm
when: saturday

If you know when the event ends, follow the when: Textag with ends:. For example:

when: September 5, 2005 5pm
ends: 7pm

You can provide a summary of the event using the what: Textag, which may come before or after when:/ends:. You can also use the following event Textags after the when:/ends:. These Textags are optional and may come in any order.

  • url: A URL for the event, e.g. a link to a site with more information
  • where: The event's location, usually an address. You can put the venue, street address, city and state on separate lines.
  • contact: Contact information, and just like location you can use several lines.

For example:

what: San Francisco Metroblogging Meet
when: Friday, August 5, 2005 7:00 PM
where: House of Shields
39 New Montgomery St.
San Francisco, CA
url: http://upcoming.org/event/25962/
